Thrissur - The Cultural Capital of Kerala

Back
Built after a rather Dutch style architecture, the Shakthan Thampuran Palace is a sight to behold.The two-storeyed complex has high roofs, thicker than usual walls, and Italian Marble on the floor.
On the outskirts of the city at a distance of some 60 km, the Athirappilly Waterfalls mark one of the best places to visit in Thrissur. Standing 80 ft tall and about 330 ft wide, these falls are the largest in the state of Kerala.
A University of Art and Culture, Kerala Kalamandir is a Government of India recognized institution dedicated to providing learning in the many performing arts native to India.
The Guruvayur Sri Krishna Temple stands as a true testimony of not only the devotion of the people of the state but also of the fine arts and crafts that Kerala is well known for.
The Dolours Basilica, is a distinct building in the city of Thrissur.It is the third tallest church in Asia and the largest church in the country with double storeyed aisles.
Claimed to be built in the year 629 AD, the Cheraman Juma Masjid is the first Mosque in Indian. It was built by the then Chera King of Kerala, Cherman Perumal.
