Trivandrum - The Evergreen City of India

Back
Padmanabhaswamy Temple has lent its names to the capital city as ‘Thiru’ ‘Anantha’ ‘Puram’, which means Sacred Abode of Lord Anantha Padmanabha. Dravidian style of architecture is prominent in this holy structure.
Epicentre of tourist flow, Kovalam Beach needs no introduction. This water delight seizes the top position in the list of tourist places in Trivandrum. Often crowded with both local and foreign tourists, it is located 13km away from the city.
Shanghumukham Beach is an ideal place for those seeking peace and serenity. Far from the hustle and bustle of the city, this place is known for breath-taking sunrises and sunsets.
Neyyar Dam and its neighbouring wildlife sanctuary - built in 1958 is a mesmerizing landscape. It is built where rivers like Neyyar, Kallar and Mullayar confluence.
Ponmudi is situated 915 meters above sea level. Its narrow, meandering pathways, dark woods and soothing climate make it a splendid location.
Kanakannu Palace, an embodiment of excellent craftsmanship and architecture built by Sree Moolan Thirunal for holding royal ceremonies and banquets.
